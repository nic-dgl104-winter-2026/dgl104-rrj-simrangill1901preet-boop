# Week 10: MV* Patterns and Beginning Open Source Contribution
## Tasks Completed

During Week 10, I attended the class and focused on understanding MV* architectural patterns, including MVC, MVVM, and MVP. Compared to the design patterns from last week, these felt more like overall structures that define how an entire application is organized rather than solving a specific problem (Blacquiere, 2026).

From the lecture and materials, I learned that:

The Model handles the data and logic
The View represents the user interface
The third component (Controller, ViewModel, or Presenter) connects the two

At first, I found it a bit confusing to differentiate between MVC and MVVM, but after thinking about examples, it became clearer. MVC felt more straightforward, while MVVM seemed more modern and suitable for mobile applications.

In addition to learning about MV* patterns, I completed the activity related to assessing external community contribution guidelines. I went back to one of the repositories I had explored in the previous week and accessed it through the provided links. From there, I carefully read through the README file and other related documents such as contributing guidelines and code of conduct (where available).

While going through this, I tried to understand what steps are expected from contributors before making any changes. For example, I noticed that most repositories expect contributors to first fork the project, create a separate branch, and then make changes instead of working directly on the main branch. Some repositories also included instructions on writing clear commit messages and properly describing changes.

I also explored one of the issues listed in the repository and tried to understand what kind of work would be required to solve it. Even though I did not fully complete a contribution, I spent time analyzing the issue and thinking about how I would approach solving it step by step.

This activity helped me understand that contributing is not just about writing code, but also about following structured processes and respecting how a project is organized.

I also started organizing my ideas for a CONTRIBUTING.md document in my journal, where I summarized what I learned about contribution processes and how I plan to approach my selected issue.

## What I Learned

This week helped me understand how important application structure is in software development. MV* patterns showed me that building an app is not just about writing code, but also about organizing different parts of the system in a clear and maintainable way.

I learned that separating the Model and View allows developers to make changes more easily without affecting the entire system. For example, updating the user interface does not necessarily require changing the core logic of the application.

I also gained a better understanding of how open-source contribution works in real-world projects. There are clear processes that need to be followed, such as forking repositories, creating branches, and following contribution guidelines. This made me realize that contributing is not only a technical task but also a structured and collaborative process.

## Reflection

In my opinion, this week was one of the more practical and realistic weeks so far. Since I attended the class, I found it easier to follow the concepts compared to previous weeks, but I still found MV* patterns slightly confusing at first, especially when trying to clearly understand the difference between MVC and MVVM.

What helped me the most was relating the concept to real-world apps. For example, I tried to think about how a music app like Spotify separates its data, interface, and functionality. This made the idea much clearer in my mind and helped me understand how these patterns are actually used.

The open-source contribution part was both interesting and slightly intimidating. Reading real project documentation made me realize how detailed and organized real-world projects are. At the same time, I felt unsure about whether I would be able to contribute properly.

One thing that became clear to me this week is that contributing is not just about solving a problem, but also about understanding how a project is structured and following its rules properly.

The most challenging part for me was understanding how to actually start contributing, because there are many steps involved. To deal with this, I decided to focus on learning the process step by step instead of trying to do everything at once.

Overall, this week helped me move closer to real-world development practices. I now have a better understanding of how applications are structured and how developers collaborate on projects. Moving forward, I want to continue working on my contribution and try to complete at least one small issue to build my confidence.
